# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Women with ambition who want to follow through, not just dream. They use Phasr during intentional moments — morning planning, end-of-day reflection, a hard week when they need to know what to do next. They are self-directed but benefit from structure. They have goals that span months, not just a to-do list for today. They want accountability from a system that respects their intelligence and doesn't perform wellness at them.

## Product Purpose

Phasr turns a life vision into a working system: phases, pillars, weekly focus, daily tasks, streaks, journaling, accountability rooms, and Sage — an AI coach who knows your context. It exists because having a vision is not the same as executing one. Success looks like a user who knows exactly what she's doing today and why it connects to where she's going.

## Brand Personality

Aesthetic but with teeth. Phasr speaks like a woman who has figured herself out and wants to help you do the same — warm but direct, feminine but not soft. It celebrates your progress without being performative about it. Less motivational poster, more trusted friend who holds you accountable. Think Pinterest meets execution: visually rich and beautiful, but built for women who actually finish things.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate productivity** (Notion, Linear, Asana): cold, utilitarian, designed for work teams — Phasr is personal, not professional-tool-shaped
- **Hustle-culture fitness apps** (Nike Run, Strava): aggressive gamification, leaderboards, performance pressure — Phasr motivates without punishing
- **Ambient wellness apps** (Calm, Headspace): too soft, too passive, too background — Phasr is structured action, not a mood board
- **Generic self-help aesthetic**: motivational posters, gold serif on cream, generic "live your best life" energy — Phasr has teeth

## Design Principles

1. **Beautiful with intention** — aesthetics earn their place by serving the system; decoration that doesn't aid clarity gets cut
2. **Direct over decorative** — UI copy, feedback states, and Sage responses should be honest and clear, not fluffy or performative
3. **Accountability before celebration** — show progress and gaps truthfully; the system holds you, it doesn't just cheer for you
4. **Feminine without softness** — embrace richness, warmth, and visual confidence without becoming passive or ambient
5. **Structure creates freedom** — organized, readable layouts that make the next step unmistakable, even on a hard day

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Every animation and transition must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative. Built for women; should feel culturally inclusive rather than defaulting to a generic neutral voice.
