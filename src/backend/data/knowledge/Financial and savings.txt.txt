Proven Strategies: Save, Invest, Achieve Freedom
Saving Basics (Budgets Are Sexy Vibes): Track every kobo—50/30/20 rule (needs/wants/savings). Nigerian hacks: local markets over Shoprite (30% cheaper produce), negotiate DSTV bills, cancel unused subs (Netflix duplicates). Build 3-6 months emergency fund in high-yield accounts (Optimus at 20%+). Cowrywise blogs stress auto-debits to beat temptation.
Investing Smart (Nigerian-Focused): Start small—₦10K mutual funds (Stanbic IBTC), stocks via Bamboo, or gov’t bonds. Diversify: 40% fixed income, 30% equities, 20% dollar assets, 10% alternatives. JollofPlus lists like Finance Blog Naija push compound interest: ₦100K at 15% annual grows to ₦1M in 17 years. Risk-rule: age in bonds (young? 80% stocks).
Debt & Freedom Path (Mr. Money Mustache Mindset): Avalanche method—high-interest first (e.g., 30% loans). FI formula: Net worth 25x annual expenses. Phases: Phase 1 emergency fund, Phase 2 debt zero, Phase 3 invest 15% salary, Phase 4 50%+ savings. Early retirement habits: no new cars, geo-arbitrage (rural living), side hustles (₦300K salary → ₦70K invest like Ada’s example).
