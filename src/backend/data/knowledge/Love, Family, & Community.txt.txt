Psychological Foundations: Bonds That Bind & Build
Relationships hinge on Sternberg’s Triangular Theory: intimacy (closeness/trust), passion (attraction/desire), commitment (long-term dedication)—balanced “consummate love” predicts endurance, per research where passionate couples marry faster. Attachment theory shapes this: secure bonds from childhood foster adult vulnerability; insecure ones spark jealousy/fear, fixable via mindful communication. Family-community links amplify wellbeing—meta-analyses show school-family partnerships cut child stress, while civic engagement (community projects) builds cross-household pride. Everyday Feminism stresses equity: active listening, bias checks, and shared power prevent toxicity, enhancing emotional granularity.
